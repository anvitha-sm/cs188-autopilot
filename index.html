01 — Motivation

The Gap Between
Autonomy and Agency

Autonomous vehicles are getting good at routine driving — but they still fall apart when the situation calls for something a little more human.

Rigid Decision-Making
In 2023, a Waymo robotaxi in San Francisco blocked a responding fire truck by sitting stationary in an intersection. The vehicle was following its safety protocols correctly — it just had no way to read the broader context of the situation. That gap between rule-following and situational judgment is still a real problem.
Cumbersome Override Interfaces
When a passenger wants to change lanes, adjust speed, or reroute, they are usually stuck with physical controls or complicated menus that interrupt the whole experience. These interfaces weren't designed with natural human behavior in mind, and it shows.
The Shared Autonomy Gap
Most systems force a binary choice — either the car is driving or you are. But real-world use demands something in between: a continuum where humans can step in and guide while the machine handles the execution. That middle ground is largely unexplored.
Our Approach
We built a system that lets users communicate through natural hand gestures and spoken commands, mapped directly to vehicle behavior in real time. Safety constraints act as guardrails so that human input enhances the system rather than creating new risks.
02 — Architecture

Two Subsystems,
One Control Loop

Autonomous Vehicle

Planning
A* Route Planner
Graph-based path search over junction nodes. Cost = road segment length + Euclidean heuristic.
Navigation
Waypoint Tracker
Interpolates lane-centered waypoints along straight and curved segments with pure-pursuit steering.
Perception
Lane Detection
OpenCV HSV thresholding on yellow/white markings + column histogram for lane position.
Perception
Obstacle Detection
MediaPipe EfficientDet; bounding-box vertical position as geometric depth proxy.
SHARED
CONTROL
LOOP

Human Interaction

Input
Gesture Recognizer
MediaPipe hand landmarks on webcam. Closed fist to stop, thumbs up for curb lane, thumbs down for inner lane. Runs in a background thread.
Input
Speech Commands
"faster" / "slower" adjusts speed by +/- 1 m/s within the allowed range.
Safety
Priority and Constraints
Fist gesture stop overrides everything. Then obstacle avoidance. Then human lane change. Then autonomous navigation. Lane boundary checks block unsafe commands.

06 — Results

System Performance

90%
Autonomous Navigation Goal Completion Rate
18 of 20 trials reached the destination. The two failures involved the vehicle circling due to an off-lane waypoint generation edge case.
~15%
Average Obstacle Collision Rate
Across 20 trials with 9–11 obstacles each. Around 60% of collisions occurred because the vehicle perceived the obstacle but began the lane change too late.
<1s
Average Gesture Recognition Latency
Fist: 0.64s avg · Thumbs up: 0.68s avg · Thumbs down: 0.63s avg. All measured from gesture display to action start.

Speech Recognition

Tested over 20 trials per command. No illegal commands (above/below speed limit) were ever executed. "Slower" had one misrecognition as "lower".
